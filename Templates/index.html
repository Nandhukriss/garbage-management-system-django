Waste Management

A waste management system is the strategy an organization uses to dispose, reduce, reuse, and prevent waste. Possible waste disposal methods are recycling, composting, incineration, landfills, bioremediation, waste to energy, and waste minimization.

Recycling

Recycling of waste is defined in the Waste Framework Directive as any recovery operation by which waste materials are reprocessed into products, materials or substances whether for the original or other purposes

FAQ's

A garbage management system refers to a comprehensive approach to handling, collecting, transporting, treating, and disposing of waste in an efficient, environmentally friendly, and sustainable manner.

Proper garbage management is crucial for maintaining public health, preventing environmental pollution, conserving resources, and promoting sustainable development. It helps minimize the impact of waste on ecosystems, reduces greenhouse gas emissions, and protects natural habitats.

The garbage management system typically involves several stages, including waste collection, segregation, transportation, treatment, and disposal.
